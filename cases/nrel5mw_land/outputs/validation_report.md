## 5MW Land-Based Reference: Coefficient Comparison Report

Findings from running [`cases/nrel5mw_land/run.py`](../run.py) against the
ElastoDyn deck bundled at
`docs/OpenFAST_files/r-test/glue-codes/openfast/5MW_Land_DLL_WTurb/`
(main + tower) and `5MW_Baseline/` (shared blade definition). The 5MW
land-based reference baseline is the de-facto open-source benchmark for
utility-scale wind-turbine simulation; its ElastoDyn deck has shipped with
the OpenFAST regression-test corpus essentially unchanged for years and
predates current systems-engineering optimisation pipelines. The
polynomial coefficients in the bundled `.dat` files were generated by a
specific BModes run at deck creation time and have not been regenerated
since.

The point of this report is parallel to the IEA-3.4 case: record the
evidence base that **the polynomial coefficients shipped in the 5MW
land-based ElastoDyn deck are not reproducible from those files' own
structural inputs by a standalone modal solve, and that mismatch is *not*
an artefact of the polynomial-basis conditioning.**

### Configuration

- Tower model: `Tower.from_elastodyn(NRELOffshrBsline5MW_Onshore_ElastoDyn.dat)`
  — RNA lumped at the tower top via the rigid-body parallel-axis assembly
  in `_tower_top_assembly_mass`. Land-based: no platform, no soil
  compliance.
- Blade model: `RotatingBlade.from_elastodyn(...)` solved at 0 rpm and at
  rated 12.1 rpm.
- Polynomial fits: `compute_tower_params_report` (FA1/FA2/SS1/SS2) and
  `compute_blade_params` (BldFl1/BldFl2/BldEdg).
- Geometry from the deck: `TipRad = 63.0 m`, `HubRad = 1.5 m`,
  `TowerHt = 87.6 m`, `HubHt(derived) = 90.0 m`,
  `NacMass = 240,000 kg`, `HubMass = 56,780 kg`.

### Classification

Mode identification is unambiguous in modes 1–4. The DOF-participation
table shows zero torsion contamination at the bending frequencies where
the ElastoDyn polynomials are fit:

| Mode | Freq (Hz) | p_FA | p_SS | p_tor | Label |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|:---|
| 1 | 0.3354 | 0.942 | 0.058 | 0.000 | FA |
| 2 | 0.3354 | 0.059 | 0.941 | 0.000 | SS |
| 3 | 2.7836 | 0.000 | 1.000 | 0.000 | SS |
| 4 | 2.9656 | 1.000 | 0.000 | 0.000 | FA |
| 5 | 6.5851 | 0.000 | 1.000 | 0.000 | SS |
| 6 | 8.0783 | 1.000 | 0.000 | 0.000 | FA |
| 7 | 12.2501 | 0.000 | 1.000 | 0.000 | SS |
| 8 | 14.2718 | 0.913 | 0.000 | 0.087 | FA (mild twist) |
| 9 | 22.1794 | 0.000 | 1.000 | 0.000 | SS |

**No degenerate FA/SS pair.** Unlike the IEA-3.4 deck, modes 1 and 2 of
the 5MW are essentially pure FA and pure SS (94 % participation each)
even though the tower stiffness is symmetric (`TwFAStif = TwSSStif`).
The 5MW RNA has a larger downwind c.m. offset (`NacCMxn = 1.9 m`,
`OverHang = -5.0 m`) than the IEA-3.4 RNA, which numerically separates
the otherwise degenerate eigenvalues — though to 4-digit frequency
precision both still report `0.3354 Hz`.

**No torsion contamination** in modes 1–7. Mild twist participation
(8.7 %) appears in mode 8 and grows to 49.6 % by mode 10, but those
modes sit far above the FA2 / SS2 selection points and do not affect
the polynomial fits we report on.

**Classifier selections.** FA1 = mode 1, FA2 = mode 4, SS1 = mode 2,
SS2 = mode 3. The pyBmodes fits on these four modes are essentially
exact: `fit_rms` = 0.0000, 0.0024, 0.0000, 0.0023 — orders of magnitude
inside the 0.09 good-fit threshold. **Mode 6** (the next FA candidate
in frequency order, the true 3rd FA bending mode) had `fit_rms = 0.0686`,
just below the 0.09 cutoff — within ~25 % of being rejected. That's a
worth-knowing margin if the threshold ever needs tightening.

### Residual analysis

The decisive diagnostic is again the *file polynomial residual*:
evaluate the file's (C₂…C₆) polynomial at the same span stations as
our pyBmodes mode shape and compute the RMS difference. A small ratio
of `file rms* / pyB rms*` means the file polynomial is a re-parametrisation
of essentially the same shape. A large ratio means it was fit to a
materially different shape.

| Mode | pyBmodes fit residual | File-poly residual against our shape | Ratio |
|:---|---:|---:|---:|
| TwFAM1Sh | 0.0000 | 0.0081 | **∞** (pyBmodes fit is essentially exact) |
| TwFAM2Sh | 0.0024 | 5.0783 | **2,116 ×** |
| TwSSM1Sh | 0.0000 | 0.0075 | **∞** |
| TwSSM2Sh | 0.0023 | 5.9009 | **2,565 ×** |
| BldFl1Sh @ 12.1 rpm | 0.0008 | 0.0020 | 2.5 × |
| BldFl2Sh @ 12.1 rpm | 0.0036 | 0.0090 | 2.5 × |
| BldEdgSh @ 12.1 rpm | 0.0002 | 0.0020 | 10 × |
| BldFl1Sh @ 0 rpm | 0.0008 | 0.0069 | 9 × |
| BldFl2Sh @ 0 rpm | 0.0037 | 0.0112 | 3 × |
| BldEdgSh @ 0 rpm | 0.0002 | 0.0013 | 6 × |

For context: a normalised mode shape on `[0, 1]` has unit tip value, so
an RMS residual of 0.01 corresponds to a 1 % shape error; 0.1 to 10 %;
1.0 means the polynomial does not approximate the shape *at all*; 5+
means the polynomial diverges by hundreds of percent of the tip value
in some span region.

The 5MW 2nd-mode tower file residuals (5.08 and 5.90) are an
order of magnitude worse than the IEA-3.4 ones (0.72 and 1.55). The
file polynomials for the 5MW 2nd FA / SS modes do not approximate any
recognisable 2nd bending shape on this structural model. Looking at
the raw coefficients to confirm:

```
TwFAM2Sh file:    [-70.5, -63.8, +289.7, -176.5,  +22.1]
TwFAM2Sh pyBmodes: [-42.2, +25.7,  +32.2,   +0.1,  -14.8]
```

C₃ has flipped sign and C₄ differs by ~9 ×; the polynomials describe
fundamentally different shapes. Same pattern for `TwSSM2Sh` with even
larger absolute coefficients.

For 1st-mode coefficients the gap is smaller in absolute terms (file
RMS ≈ 0.008 vs essentially-zero pyBmodes RMS), but the RATIO is
infinite — pyBmodes' polynomial fits its own shape exactly to the
limit of double precision, so any nonzero file residual is "infinitely
worse" by ratio. The shape error itself is ~1 %, comparable to the
IEA-3.4 case.

### Conclusion

- **1st-mode file residuals are ~ 0.008** (TwFAM1Sh = 0.0081,
  TwSSM1Sh = 0.0075). These are credible polynomial representations
  of *some* mode shape — order-of-magnitude shape error is ~ 1 %, but
  not a re-parametrisation of the shape we computed. pyBmodes fits its
  own shape to ≤ 1 part in 10,000.
- **2nd-mode file residuals are 5.08 (TwFAM2Sh) and 5.90 (TwSSM2Sh)**
  — over 2,000 × worse than our fits. These polynomials cannot be
  interpreted as 2nd bending mode descriptions on the 5MW structural
  model in the deck.
- **Vandermonde-conditioning hypothesis rejected for all 7 mode types
  examined here.** Even the smallest blade-mode residual ratio is 2.5 ×,
  and the tower 2nd-mode ratios are in the thousands. There is no
  near-equivalent-fit case here (unlike IEA-3.4's BldFl2Sh, where the
  ratio was ~ 1).
- **Disagreement is more dramatic than IEA-3.4.** The 5MW deck's 2nd-
  mode tower residuals are roughly 7 × worse (5.08 vs 0.72; 5.90 vs
  1.55). The 5MW deck is older and has gone through more cycles of
  structural-property refinement without re-running BModes against the
  current `.dat` files.

**Most likely cause of the disagreement:** the polynomial coefficients
in the 5MW `.dat` files were produced by a specific BModes run at the
time the deck was first published, against a then-current set of tower
and blade properties. The structural property tables in the `.dat`
files have been edited since (mass density tweaks, EI distribution
refinements), but no one has re-run BModes and patched the polynomial
coefficients to match. The 2nd-mode coefficients look like they could
be from an even earlier revision still — the disagreement is too large
to be explained by a small parameter perturbation.

**Counterfactual checks already done:**

- It is *not* a wrong-mode selection. Modes 1–4 are pure FA / SS with
  zero torsion participation, and frequencies sit at the right values
  (1st pair at 0.3354 Hz, 2nd pair at ~ 2.8–3.0 Hz). Modes 8 and 10
  carry twist contamination but are not selected.
- It is *not* a centrifugal-stiffening mismatch on the blade. The 0 rpm
  and 12.1 rpm comparisons give similar file-residual patterns; the
  stiffening shift just adds a few percent (see §Frequencies).
- It is *not* a Vandermonde basis-conditioning issue. If it were, the
  file-residual and pyBmodes-residual columns would be of comparable
  magnitude even when the coefficients themselves differ. Instead the
  ratios run from 2.5 × to 2,565 ×.
- It is *not* an axis-convention swap. Modes 1 (FA) and 2 (SS) are
  directly identifiable from participation, the classifier picks them
  unambiguously, and the BMI tower-frame axis convention has been
  unit-tested against the cert-test cases.

### Frequencies

pyBmodes computed frequencies (Hz):

| Mode | Tower (n=10) | Blade @ 0 rpm (n=8) | Blade @ 12.1 rpm (n=8) |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | 0.3354 | 0.6460 | 0.7185 |
| 2 | 0.3354 | 1.0720 | 1.0782 |
| 3 | 2.7836 | 1.9666 | 2.0177 |
| 4 | 2.9656 | 4.0125 | 4.0238 |
| 5 | 6.5851 | 4.5498 | 4.6044 |
| 6 | 8.0783 | 8.0771 | 8.1316 |
| 7 | 12.2501 | 9.4159 | 9.4377 |
| 8 | 14.2718 | 12.7382 | 12.7955 |

**Centrifugal stiffening on the blade** shifts the 1st-flap frequency
from 0.6460 Hz (0 rpm) to 0.7185 Hz (12.1 rpm), i.e. + 11.2 %.
The 1st-edge frequency moves from 1.0720 → 1.0782 Hz (+ 0.6 %), and
the 2nd-flap from 1.9666 → 2.0177 Hz (+ 2.6 %). Larger first-flap shift
than IEA-3.4's + 4.0 % — the 5MW rotor speed is faster (12.1 vs 10.5
rpm) and the flap stiffness lower at this scale.

**File-implied frequencies are not directly extractable** from the
shipped `.dat`. ElastoDyn does not store modal frequencies — they are
*outputs* of an OpenFAST run. We can compare against external
references for sanity:

- **Bir 2010, Table 1, BModes column** for the 5MW land-based tower
  with rigid head mass: 1st FA = 0.3324 Hz. Our 0.3354 is **0.9 %
  high** — well within the 2 % bound enforced by
  `test_5mw_tower_frequency_target` in
  [`tests/test_elastodyn_reader.py`](../../../tests/test_elastodyn_reader.py).
- **Jonkman et al. 2009, Table 9-1** (coupled FAST simulation):
  1st tower FA = 0.32 Hz. Our 0.3354 is **4.8 %** above this; the gap
  is the standard rigid-RNA-vs-coupled-multibody offset and is the
  same effect we already documented in the standalone test rationale.

### Implication for the README

The single sentence the README can claim with full backing from this
report:

> The polynomial-coefficient blocks shipped in the `_ElastoDyn.dat`
> files of the 5MW land-based reference baseline cannot be reproduced
> from the structural-property blocks in the same files via a standalone
> modal solve. The disagreement is more severe than what we observe on
> the IEA-3.4-130-RWT deck: 1st-mode shape errors of ~ 1 % and 2nd-mode
> RMS gaps of 5.08–5.90 — i.e. the bundled 2nd-FA and 2nd-SS polynomials
> do not approximate the 2nd bending modes of the bundled tower
> structural inputs at all.

Combined with the IEA-3.4 finding, this is now two independent
turbines from two independent upstream pipelines (an auto-generation
pipeline and a hand-crafted BModes-era pipeline) showing the same
pattern. The next natural step is to verify the same effect on a third
deck (e.g. IEA-10, IEA-15, IEA-22, or 25 MW upscale) to harden the
"ecosystem-wide" framing, and then to write the user-facing README
material citing the cross-turbine evidence.
